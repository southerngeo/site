Drone Site Assessments

Drones can aid in site assessments by providing high-resolution aerial imagery and 3D mapping. This up-to-date data can be used to assess site characteristics such as:

  • topography
  • vegetation
  • infrastructure
  • potential hazards
  • environmental risks

Drones are a cost-effective and efficient way to gather detailed information about a site, enabling better decision-making and planning for development and land use.

Drone Georeferenced Aerial Imagery

Drone aerial imagery can be better than satellite imagery due to its higher resolution, flexibility and control, up-to-date data, and cost-effectiveness for small areas. It can provide more detailed and clearer images, be adjusted for specific needs, and captured and processed more quickly.

Georeferenced aerial imagery can overlay on top of existing maps, giving great accuracy and up-to-date data on work sites and areas of interest.

3D Software Development

Clients or stakeholders often want to see 3D visualizations because they provide a more immersive and realistic experience compared to 2D images or videos. 3D visualizations can help to better convey:

  • depth
  • scale
  • texture
  • density

of objects or environments, which can be particularly important in areas such as planning, ecology, forestry, and infrastructure.

They also allow for greater interactivity and customization, enabling viewers to explore and interact with objects from various angles and perspectives. This can help to improve understanding and engagement, making 3D visualizations a powerful tool for communication, education, and marketing.
